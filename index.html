Identity

THE MILANO OUTLOOK

We do not copy templates; we draft new principles of student engineering and design efficiency.

The Outfit

Milano Racing is an elite engineering outfit composed of six student innovators representing the GIIS Punggol Smart Campus in Singapore. Taking cues from the Milano, the legendary starship engineered to break interstellar speed barriers, we are dedicated to building a miniature F1 machine that moves faster than physically expected.

The Methodology

We translate mathematical theories into racetrack dominance. Using advanced computational fluid dynamics (CFD), parametric CAD modelling and industrial CNC manufacturing, our goal is absolute track speed. Beyond fabrication, we operate as a cohesive corporate structure, managing marketing, design and financing with technical precision.

The Mission

To forge the next generation of engineers, leaders and scientific innovators. Milano Racing is a testing ground for growth, discipline and scientific inquiry. We don't just compete on the track; we build relationships and integrations that shape sustainable high-performance design.

Sequence

THE ENGINEERING TIMELINE

From initial flow dynamics calculations to victory on the 20-meter sprint track.

I
Stage 01

Aerodynamic Profiling

Analyzing flow dynamics, studying lift forces and drafting initial nosecone and wing profiles based on physics formulas.

II
Stage 02

CAD Modeling & Assembly

Creating exact parametric 3D models of wheels, bodies, wings and bearings inside modern CAD software ensuring exact assembly tolerances.

III
Stage 03

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Running high-precision fluid dynamics simulations on virtual wind tunnels. We analyze turbulence, drag coefficients and downforce distribution.

IV
Stage 04

CNC Manufacturing

Precision manufacturing using CNC milling machines, carving the car out of lightweight substrates. 3D printing custom front/rear wings.

V
Stage 05

Testing & Verification

Deploying the physical car on a 20-meter test track. Analyzing gate speeds, laser timing and structure wear for final modifications.
